Disability Scholar & Activist

Pharmableism (a term I coined): While ableism takes many forms, the specific form of ableism affecting medical drugs is pharmableism: the belief that pharmaceuticals can, and therefore should, be used to cure ailments such as a disease, disorder, syndrome, or disability. This belief operates on a preceding belief that there exists an ideal bodymind to which we should strive for and that bodyminds that skew from the social imaginaries’ ideal are bad.

About

I am a Graduate student at American University specializing in the ethical and social implications of Disability, Gender, Race, and Technology.

Research

My research focuses on the intersection of disability studies, gender, race, and technology. I examine how these fields intersect to shape experiences of inclusion and exclusion in health care, education, and in digital spaces for disabled communities.
