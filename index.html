Pharmableism (a term I coined)

Arising from a medical theory of disability, pharmableism is the belief that pharmaceuticals can, and therefore should, be used to cure ailments such as a disease, disorder, syndrome, or disability. Necessary to this belief is the existence of the ideal bodymind within the social imaginary, of which we strive for. Under pharmableism, bodyminds that skew from the social imaginaries' ideal are bad and can be 'corrected' with pharmaceutical intervention.
