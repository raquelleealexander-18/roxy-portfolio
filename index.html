About

I am a Graduate student in the Department of Philosophy and Religion at American University. My work explores the vital intersections of Disability, Gender, Race, and Technology, focusing on how systemic structures can be reimagined through both ethical inquiry and direct action.

My activism is deeply rooted in mutual aid and community-building, specifically aimed at improving the lives of disabled individuals and the unhoused community.

Research

Technological Accessibility & Race

Investigating the digital divide and the racialized nature of assistive technologies in the 21st century.

Analyzing feminist philosophical frameworks to redefine autonomy within disabled communities.
